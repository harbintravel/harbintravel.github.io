Harbin’s Best Food Blogs and Influencers to Follow

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

If you're planning a trip to Harbin or simply want to explore its culinary scene from afar, following the right food bloggers and influencers is a must. Known for its rich Russian influences, hearty Northeastern Chinese cuisine, and iconic winter delicacies, Harbin offers a food culture unlike any other in China. From steaming hot pots to crispy guo bao rou (sweet and sour pork), the city’s flavors are as bold as its ice sculptures.

To help you navigate Harbin’s food scene, we’ve compiled a list of the best food blogs and social media influencers who capture the essence of the city’s gastronomy. Whether you're looking for hidden street food gems or high-end dining experiences, these experts will guide you through Harbin’s most delicious offerings.

Top Harbin Food Blogs You Should Bookmark

1. The Frozen Feast

A blog dedicated to Harbin’s winter culinary traditions, The Frozen Feast dives deep into seasonal specialties like bingtanghulu (candied fruit skewers) and suancai (fermented cabbage) hot pot. The blog features detailed reviews of local eateries, interviews with chefs, and even DIY recipes for those who want to recreate Harbin’s flavors at home.

2. Harbin Eats

Run by a lifelong Harbin resident, Harbin Eats is a treasure trove of insider knowledge. The blog covers everything from the best jiaozi (dumpling) spots to where to find authentic Russian-style bread. The writer’s personal anecdotes and historical tidbits make each post engaging and informative.

3. Northeastern Noms

While not exclusively focused on Harbin, Northeastern Noms offers fantastic coverage of the region’s cuisine, with Harbin as a recurring highlight. Expect mouthwatering photography, detailed food guides, and comparisons between Harbin’s dishes and those of neighboring cities like Shenyang and Changchun.

Must-Follow Harbin Food Influencers on Social Media

1. @HarbinFoodie (Instagram & Douyin)

This influencer’s vibrant feed is a visual love letter to Harbin’s street food. From sizzling chuan’r (skewers) to creamy lao bing (pancakes), every post is a feast for the eyes. Their reels often feature quick reviews and behind-the-scenes looks at food vendors.

2. @RussianInHarbin (Weibo & Xiaohongshu)

A unique perspective comes from this Russian expat who explores Harbin’s fusion cuisine. Their content highlights Russian-influenced dishes like hongchang (red sausage) and lieba (black bread), offering cultural context alongside food reviews.

3. @DumplingKing (YouTube & Bilibili)

If you’re a fan of Harbin’s dumpling culture, this influencer is your go-to. Their videos showcase everything from high-end jiaozi restaurants to family-run shops where the dough is still rolled by hand.

Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

Best Street Food Stalls in Harbin

  • Zhongyang Street’s Night Market – A must-visit for tanghulu, grilled squid, and malatang.
  • Daoli District’s Breakfast Alley – Famous for you tiao (fried dough sticks) and warm soy milk.

Underrated Restaurants Loved by Locals

  • Lao Chang Spring Pancakes – A no-frills spot serving the crispiest chunbing (spring pancakes) in town.
  • Dongfang Jiaozi Wang – A humble eatery praised for its juicy lamb dumplings.

Seasonal Delicacies You Can’t Miss

Harbin’s food scene shifts dramatically with the seasons. In winter, warm up with yangrou paomo (lamb stew with bread), while summer calls for refreshing liangpi (cold noodles). Follow these bloggers and influencers to stay updated on what’s in season during your visit.

Final Bite

Harbin’s food culture is as dynamic as its famous Ice Festival. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned foodie, these blogs and influencers will ensure you don’t miss a single bite. From viral street snacks to centuries-old recipes, they’ll guide you through the best of what Harbin has to offer.
