What Are the Opening Hours and Ticket Prices for Harbin Ice and Snow World?

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Harbin Ice and Snow World (冰雪大世界, Bīngxuě Dà Shìjiè) is the centerpiece of the Harbin International Ice and Snow Festival. Located in Songbei District on the northern bank of the Songhua River, it's one of the world’s largest ice theme parks, showcasing enormous illuminated ice castles, sculptures, ice slides, and interactive winter attractions. Below is a comprehensive guide to the park’s opening hours, ticket prices, and visitor tips.

⏰ Opening Dates & Hours (2024–2026)

  • Opening period:

    • Starts mid to late December (typically from around December 15–23).

    • Extends through late February or early March—usually until weather forces closure zh.wikipedia.org+8chinadiscovery.com+8trip.com+8.

  • Daily hours:

    • On opening day (e.g., Dec 23), typically open from 4:00 PM to 9:30 PM .

    • From the following day until closing, regular daily hours are 11:00 AM to 9:30 PM .

For the upcoming 2025–26 festival, an expected start would be around mid-December 2025, with regular hours from 11:00–21:30 or 22:00, closing sometime in February 2026 travelchinaguide.com+2chinadiscovery.com+2travelchinaguide.com+2.

🎟 Ticket Pricing

Tickets generally follow standard pricing tiers:

  • Single-entry adult ticket: CNY 328 (about $45–50) icefestivalharbin.com+4icefestivalharbin.com+4trip.com+4trip.com+3travelchinaguide.com+3travelchinaguide.com+3.

  • Discount ticket (youths, students, seniors): CNY 240 .

    • Youth discounts cover those under 18, college students under 24, local residents, the disabled, and people between 1.2–1.4 m tall trip.com+1travelchinaguide.com+1.

  • Free entrance for children under 1.2 meters tall viator.com+10travelchinaguide.com+10icefestivalharbin.com+10.

🎫 Tips for Buying Tickets

  1. Buy early – Book online or through your hotel in November or December to secure your entry .

  2. QR code tickets – Mobile tickets are common and simple: just scan to enter .

  3. Travel packages – Many tours bundle transport and admission, but check for hidden fees travelchinaguide.com+3peek.com+3viator.com+3.

  4. Avoid crowds – Visiting mid-week or early afternoon helps avoid long entry lines and ice-slide waits .

🎡 What You’ll See Inside

  • Grand ice palaces, castles, and sculptural replicas of world landmarks.

  • Colorful LED lighting at night gives everything a magical atmosphere en.wikipedia.orgtrip.com.

  • Ice slides, Ferris wheels, ice bars, and interactive snow activities trip.com.

  • Nearby Sun Island and Zhaolin Park street-level expos to expand your winter wonderland tour icefestivalharbin.com+10icefestivalharbin.com+10travelchinaguide.com+10.

🧳 Visitor Info & Recommendations

  • Dress warmly – Temperatures often dip to –20 °C (–4 °F) or lower .

  • Footwear with traction is vital—slippery surfaces are common .

  • Bring portable chargers – The cold drains batteries fast .

  • Stay hydrated and warm – Indoor warming zones, hot snacks, and drinks are available in the park .

  • Stay late – Lighting shows begin at dusk, and the night views are truly spectacular .

🌐 Quick Summary

  • Opening: Mid-Dec to Feb/Mar, typically 11 AM–9:30 PM.

  • Tickets: Adult ¥328, Discount ¥240, Kids under 1.2 m free.

  • Extras: Separate admission for Sun Island (¥330).

  • Tips: Plan early, bundle transport, go on weekdays, stay warm.

Harbin Ice and Snow World is a breathtaking winter attraction—by planning ahead and dressing for the cold, your visit is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
