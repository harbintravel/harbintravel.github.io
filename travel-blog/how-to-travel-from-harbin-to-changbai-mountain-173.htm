How to Travel from Harbin to Changbai Mountain

  • 首页

  • Travel Blog

Changbai Mountain, also known as Baekdu Mountain, is a majestic natural wonder straddling the border between China and North Korea. Renowned for its stunning Tianchi (Heaven Lake), hot springs, and rich biodiversity, this mountain is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and adventurers. If you are starting your journey in Harbin, the vibrant capital of Heilongjiang Province, there are several transportation options to consider.

1. By Train

High-Speed Trains

The most convenient and efficient way to travel from Harbin to Changbai Mountain is by high-speed train. While there isn’t a direct train to the mountain itself, you can take a train to nearby cities like Dunhua or Antu, which are gateways to Changbai Mountain.

Route Details:

  • Harbin to Dunhua: The high-speed train journey takes around 3–4 hours.
  • Dunhua to Erdaobaihe (Changbai Mountain’s nearest town): A connecting train from Dunhua to Erdaobaihe takes about 2–3 hours.

Ticket Prices:

  • Harbin to Dunhua: Tickets range from ¥150 to ¥250, depending on the seat class.
  • Dunhua to Erdaobaihe: Tickets typically cost between ¥50 and ¥100.

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros: Fast, comfortable, and relatively affordable.
  • Cons: Requires a transfer and advance booking during peak travel seasons.

Regular Trains

For budget travelers, regular trains offer a cheaper alternative. However, they take longer and might not be as comfortable as high-speed trains.

Route Details:

  • Harbin to Antu: The journey takes about 6–8 hours.
  • Antu to Erdaobaihe: Local transportation options are available, including buses and taxis.

Ticket Prices:

  • Harbin to Antu: Approximately ¥100 to ¥150 for a hard seat or hard sleeper.
  • Antu to Erdaobaihe: Costs vary depending on the mode of transport.

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros: Affordable and readily available.
  • Cons: Longer travel time and limited amenities.

2. By Bus

Direct long-distance buses from Harbin to Erdaobaihe offer another option for reaching Changbai Mountain. While buses are slower than trains, they provide direct access without the need for transfers.

Route Details:

  • Harbin to Erdaobaihe: The journey typically takes around 8–10 hours, depending on road conditions.

Ticket Prices:

  • Cost: ¥150 to ¥200 per person.

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros: Direct travel to Erdaobaihe without transfers.
  • Cons: Long travel time and potential discomfort during the journey.

Booking Tips:

  • Tickets can be purchased at Harbin’s long-distance bus station or online through local platforms.
  • Departure times are often early in the morning, so plan accordingly.

3. By Car

Driving from Harbin to Changbai Mountain offers the most flexibility, allowing you to explore the scenic countryside and stop at interesting locations along the way. The drive covers approximately 600 kilometers (373 miles) and takes about 7–8 hours.

Recommended Route:

  • Harbin → Mudanjiang → Yanji → Erdaobaihe

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros: Complete control over your schedule and the ability to explore off-the-beaten-path destinations.
  • Cons: Requires access to a car, navigation skills, and familiarity with local driving rules.

Car Rental:

  • Rental costs vary, with rates typically starting at ¥300 per day.
  • Ensure you have a valid driver’s license and necessary permits.

4. By Plane

For travelers who prioritize speed, flying is the quickest way to reach Changbai Mountain from Harbin. The closest airport to the mountain is Changbaishan Airport, located near Erdaobaihe.

Flight Details:

  • Harbin to Changbaishan Airport: The flight takes about 1 hour and 30 minutes.

Ticket Prices:

  • Cost: ¥600 to ¥1,000, depending on the airline and booking time.

Transportation from the Airport:

  • Shuttle buses and taxis are available to take you to Erdaobaihe and the Changbai Mountain Scenic Area.

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros: Fast and convenient for those with limited time.
  • Cons: Higher cost and potential delays due to weather conditions.

5. Key Stops and Attractions Along the Way

Dunhua

  • Jingbo Lake: Known for its crystal-clear waters and volcanic formations, this lake is a great stopover for nature lovers.
  • Pudacuo Temple: A serene Tibetan-style temple in the area.

Erdaobaihe

  • Hot Springs: Relax in natural hot springs before or after your hike.
  • Changbai Mountain Museum: Learn about the mountain’s history, geology, and biodiversity.

6. Tips for Visiting Changbai Mountain

  • Weather Preparation: Changbai Mountain’s weather can be unpredictable. Pack warm clothing and rain gear regardless of the season.
  • Tickets and Fees: Entrance tickets to the scenic area cost around ¥125 to ¥275, depending on the season and sections you visit.
  • Best Time to Visit: The best time to explore the mountain is from June to September when the weather is pleasant, and the scenery is lush.
  • Health and Safety: The altitude can cause mild discomfort. Stay hydrated and pace yourself during hikes.

Traveling from Harbin to Changbai Mountain offers a mix of adventure, natural beauty, and cultural exploration. Whether you prefer the speed of a plane, the comfort of a train, or the flexibility of driving, there’s a travel option to suit every type of traveler.
